Introduction

Why prevention matters

Heart disease is one of the leading causes of illness globally. Prevention through lifestyle changes, early detection and regular check-ups significantly reduces the risk. This page summarizes evidence-based prevention tips, risk factors, and when to seek medical help.

Healthy Lifestyle & Daily Habits

Simple, effective daily routines

  • Aim for 30 minutes of moderate exercise most days (brisk walking, cycling).
  • Follow a heart-healthy diet: plenty of vegetables, fruits, whole grains, lean protein.
  • Limit processed foods, red meat, sugary drinks and excessive salt.
  • Quit smoking ‚Äî ask your doctor for support programs or nicotine replacement.
  • Manage stress with mindfulness, hobbies, or professional support.

Physical Activity Guidelines

Simple exercise plan

  • Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week or 75 minutes of vigorous activity.
  • Include strength training 2 days/week (resistance bands, body-weight exercises).
  • Break long sitting sessions with short walks or stretches every hour.

Do's & Don'ts

‚úÖ Do's

üö´ Don'ts

When to See a Doctor

Seek prompt medical advice if you have:

  • Repeated chest discomfort, pressure or tightness
  • Shortness of breath not explained by exertion
  • Fainting spells, sudden weakness, or slurred speech
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat or unexplained fatigue

Emergency Warning Signs

Call emergency services immediately if someone has:

  • Severe chest pain or pressure lasting > 5 minutes
  • Fainting or loss of consciousness
  • Sudden difficulty speaking or facial droop
  • Sudden severe breathlessness
