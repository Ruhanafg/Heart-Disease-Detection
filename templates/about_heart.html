What is Heart Disease? ü´Ä

A concise overview

Heart disease is a broad term that covers various conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels. It includes disorders such as coronary artery disease (narrowing of the arteries), arrhythmias (irregular heartbeat), heart failure (pump failure), and congenital heart defects. These conditions may lead to chest pain, heart attacks, reduced exercise tolerance, or life-threatening complications.

Types of Heart Disease üß©

Common categories

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

Blockage of coronary arteries supplying the heart muscle ‚Äî often causes angina and myocardial infarction.

Arrhythmia

Abnormal heart rhythms (too fast, too slow or irregular) which can affect heart function and safety.

Heart Failure

Condition where the heart cannot pump sufficient blood to meet the body's needs (chronic or acute).

Congenital Heart Disease

Structural heart defects present from birth that may require monitoring or corrective procedures.

Valvular Heart Disease

Diseases of heart valves causing stenosis or regurgitation and impairing blood flow dynamics.

Cardiomyopathy

Diseases of heart muscle that weaken the heart and may lead to failure or arrhythmias.

Causes üîé

How heart disease develops

  • Atherosclerosis ‚Äî build-up of plaque inside arteries due to cholesterol and inflammation.
  • High blood pressure (hypertension) causing wear-and-tear on vessels and the heart.
  • Genetic predisposition and inherited syndromes.
  • Metabolic diseases such as diabetes and obesity accelerating vascular damage.
  • Infections, toxins, and some autoimmune conditions affecting heart tissue.

Symptoms ‚ö†Ô∏è

Signs to watch for

  • Chest pain (angina), discomfort, pressure or squeezing sensation.
  • Shortness of breath during activity or at rest.
  • Palpitations, light-headedness, dizziness or fainting.
  • Unexplained fatigue, weakness, reduced exercise tolerance.
  • Swelling in legs or abdomen (sign of heart failure).

Risk Factors üö®

What increases the likelihood

  • Age (risk rises with age)
  • Family history of heart disease
  • High blood pressure, high cholesterol
  • Diabetes and metabolic syndrome
  • Smoking, sedentary lifestyle, unhealthy diet
  • Excess alcohol, stress, poor sleep

Global Impact & Statistics üåç

Brief facts

Cardiovascular diseases remain the leading cause of death globally. They account for a large proportion of premature mortality and healthcare burden. Regional incidence and mortality vary; many cases are associated with modifiable risk factors and social determinants of health.

Diagnosis Methods üß™

How clinicians evaluate heart disease

  • Clinical history and physical examination
  • Electrocardiogram (ECG) to detect arrhythmia or ischemia
  • Blood tests: cardiac enzymes, lipids, glucose
  • Imaging: echocardiography, cardiac CT, MRI
  • Stress testing and coronary angiography when indicated
